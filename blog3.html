Things I wish I knew earlier

Everybody has their way of living life but not always the right way. The society in which we live mainly focuses on the wealth and achievements of an individual that they achieved. No one analyses the reason and hardships behind their success but cares about the rewards they get in return. Like sheep, everyone follows them blindly. Now, you might be thinking about what I have achieved to give a lecture to someone regarding this. But do you know to write a blog sometimes you don't need to be an achiever, you can do it for fun? Also, someone said, "I have not failed. I've just found 10,000 ways that won't work.". Surely, I will not show you the 10,000 things that will not make your life on track but some limited things that I learned and implemented as I keep growing and upskilling which will keep you on track.

BALANCE IN EVERYTHING

Balance is an important aspect of life whether it is taken in respect of eating, studying, relationship, gaming, or other things. This aspect I lacked when I was preparing for my Chartered Accountant Foundation paper. The paper has four subjects, i.e, English, Business, Maths, and Law. Each subject has 100 marks each, making it a paper of a total of 400 marks. And there were two conditions to pass the exam, i.e, get at least 40% marks in each subject to pass individually in subjects and a total of 200 of all the subjects. I was good at maths, in comparison to other subjects as the other three were theory subjects which you have to learn and I am terrible at that. So, I gave most of my time to maths leaving behind the others. The situation concluded that I was unable to pass the exam.

ONE STEP AT A TIME RULE

Most of the students don't understand the essence of achieving a long-term goal by taking step-by-step movements toward it. Sometimes, it does not matter what your pace is but it is important to start before it gets too late to begin your journey. We can break a big task into small chunks and by completing those small chunks we can make them stepping stones on our way to our main goal. This aspect was gained by me when I started learning web development. In web development, people divide their projects into small tasks for each day. These small tasks are assigned to each individual to complete. On completion of all these small tasks with everyday effort result in the creation of the project. Also, someone said, "Big things have small beginnings."
